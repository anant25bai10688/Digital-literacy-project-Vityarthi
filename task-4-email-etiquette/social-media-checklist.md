# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## DO's ✅

1. **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that accurately represents who you are academically and professionally. Recruiters and internship coordinators actively review social media.

2. **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parents, or future employer saw this?" If not, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings wisely.** Regularly review who can see your posts, stories, and tagged photos. Set personal accounts to private if you prefer to keep them separate from your professional identity.

4. **Give credit where it is due.** When sharing someone else's work, article, or image, always tag or credit the original creator. This builds trust and avoids plagiarism.

5. **Report harassment and block offenders.** If you experience or witness cyberbullying or harassment, use the platform's reporting tools immediately and, if necessary, report to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call Helpline 1930.

---

## DON'Ts ❌

1. **Do not share personal information publicly.** Avoid posting your phone number, home address, daily schedule, or location in real time. This information can be exploited by bad actors.

2. **Do not engage in or encourage cyberbullying.** This includes sharing embarrassing content about others, participating in pile-ons, spreading rumours, or liking/sharing posts that target an individual. Under the IT Act, 2000, cyberbullying is a punishable offence.

3. **Do not share unverified news or misinformation.** Before forwarding a message or post, verify it through a credible source. Sharing fake news — even unintentionally — can have serious legal and social consequences.

4. **Do not post about institutional matters without authorization.** Complaints, grievances, or internal conflicts should be resolved through proper channels — not aired publicly on social media, where they can damage your institution's reputation and your own.

5. **Do not use social media as a substitute for direct communication.** Sensitive conversations — conflicts, apologies, serious requests — should never be handled through public posts, story reactions, or mass messages. Use direct, private, and respectful communication instead.
