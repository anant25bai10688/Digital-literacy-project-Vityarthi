# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Digital Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A strong password is at least 12 characters long and combines uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across platforms.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. This adds a second layer of verification even if your password is compromised.

- [ ] **Think before you click.** Never click on links sent via unknown numbers on WhatsApp, SMS, or email — even if they appear to be from your bank, college, or a government authority. Verify through the official website or app.

- [ ] **Keep your software and apps updated.** Security patches in updates protect you from known vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates on your phone and laptop.

- [ ] **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks.** Do not log into banking apps, email, or institutional portals on café, mall, or railway station Wi-Fi without a VPN.

---

## Social Media & Communication Safety

- [ ] **Set your personal accounts to private.** Regularly review your privacy settings on Instagram, Snapchat, and other platforms. Audit who follows you and remove unknown accounts.

- [ ] **Never share OTPs, passwords, or PINs** with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank's customer care, college admin, or a government helpline. Legitimate organizations never ask for this information.

- [ ] **Screenshot and preserve evidence** if you experience cyberbullying or harassment. Do not delete messages before reporting — they serve as evidence.

---

## Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory for Indian Students)

- [ ] **Never scan a QR code or enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** You only enter your PIN when sending money, never when receiving it. This is one of the most common UPI fraud tactics used against students.

- [ ] **Verify UPI payment requests carefully.** Check the name and UPI ID of the recipient before confirming any transaction. Fraudsters often create IDs that look similar to legitimate contacts (e.g., paytm-support@upi vs paytm@upi).

---

## If You Are a Victim – How to Report

- [ ] **Report to the National Cyber Crime Portal:** Visit **cybercrime.gov.in** to file a complaint online. This is the Government of India's official portal for reporting cybercrimes, including financial fraud, cyberbullying, and identity theft.

- [ ] **Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** (available 24x7). This is a dedicated helpline for reporting online financial fraud and cybercrime incidents. Calling quickly after a fraud increases the chance of recovering lost funds.

- [ ] **Report within your college.** Inform your faculty advisor, warden, or the Student Grievance Cell if the cybercrime involves your institution or fellow students.
